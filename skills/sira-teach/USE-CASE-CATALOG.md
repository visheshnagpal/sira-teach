# Use-Case Catalog — imaginative lesson designs per subject

A menu of **"if you want to teach X, here is the imaginative way to do it"** — ~50 ready lesson archetypes plus reusable container formats, distilled from Brandon Hendrickson's **Lost Tools of Learning** archive (losttools.substack.com). Each is an Egan-grounded design a parent can adapt at home.

**How to use this file.** When a mission points at a topic, scan the matching domain for an archetype to adapt, or grab a **cross-cutting format** (bottom of file) and drop your topic into it. Then plan the actual lesson with the frameworks in [EGAN-TOOLKIT.md](./EGAN-TOOLKIT.md) and reach for retention machinery in [TEACHING-PATTERNS.md](./TEACHING-PATTERNS.md). The "materials" lines are *starting points for `RESOURCES.md`* — vet each for your child's age before trusting it.

Stage tags (**S**omatic · **M**ythic · **R**omantic · **P**hilosophic · **I**ronic) are *centers of gravity*, not age-gates — the tools overlap and layer.

## Nature & Science

**Know a Tree** — Pick one nearby tree; observe it across seasons and years with all the senses (feel bark, crush leaves, draw roots), name it like a pet, later add a contrasting tree and look up its species and "goals." *Hook:* the object→person flip — a plant is a "slow-motion animal," your distant cousin. *S→M→R→P.* *Materials:* Chamovitz *What a Plant Knows*; Mancuso *Revolutionary Genius of Plants*; Gooley *How to Read a Tree*.

**Local Birds** — Learn ~40 local species by memory on two-page notebook spreads (names, diet, predators, calls); imitate the calls. *Hook:* "we live in Jurassic Park — literal dinosaurs flit around us"; the power of naming what you see. *S→M→R(collection)→P.* *Materials:* Merlin Bird ID app; *Little Book of Backyard Bird Songs*.

**Track the Sky** — From a fixed spot, trace Sun, Moon, then planets over time; predict next month's sunset; capstone: tell time by the Moon. *Hook:* the biggest things we see but never notice (the Moon is out in daytime half the time). *S→R(wonder)→P.* *Materials:* Stellarium / Star Walk 2.

**Your Solar System is Wrong** — A single ~90-min lesson dissecting the standard picture as wrong: better words are "balls of rock / balls of air," and planets are *falling*, not floating. *Hook:* a "what's wrong with this picture?" riddle on a stupid/smart binary — real science is genuinely weird. *M core→R→P.* *Materials:* Scale of the Universe 2; xkcd.

**How to Really SEE an Eclipse** — Use metaphor not numbers to *feel* that the Sun is huge and far: swell up until Earth is a tennis ball, the Moon a pea, the Sun a two-story house; toss peas at the house. *Hook:* "numbers numb the mind"; the shock that a pea blocks a house. *R(extremes)+M(metaphor).* *Materials:* xkcd #1877; Josh Worth "If the Moon Were Only 1 Pixel."

**Plant MatchMaker** — Paste a long prompt into ChatGPT with a plant photo; it IDs the plant, then *personifies* it — casts it as a character, tells its evolutionary backstory and how to nurture or kill it. *Hook:* know a plant "the way I'd know a person." *M(personification)→P.* *Template for Eganizing any AI task.*

**Secrets & Revelations (science)** — Build each lesson around one mind-flipping fact but *withhold* it as a secret to be earned (e.g. "you are literally a fish in a bag"). *Hook:* scarcity → value. *S→M→R→P.* (See the pattern of the same name in TEACHING-PATTERNS.)

**A Fossil Hunt to End the Culture War** — Help skeptics *try to disprove* evolution: contests to find a fossil in the "wrong" layer or build the best flood model, paired with recorded cross-divide conversations. *Hook:* the heroism of staying in conversation; anomalies as gold. *R/P (falsification).* *Materials:* rockd.org; Strevens *The Knowledge Machine*.

**Cryptids / UFOs / Bayes** — Study rumored creatures or alien evidence with responsible books, argue the evidence, then do real Bayesian calculations with "Bayes boxes." *Hook:* "cryptids are training wheels for the rational mind"; the adolescent lust for the strange. *M↔P bridge→I.* *Materials:* Loxton & Prothero *Abominable Science!*; *Wild Thing* podcast.

**We are Surrounded by Mystery** — Whatever you teach, name the genuine mysteries inside it (past, current, or permanent — consciousness, time). *Hook:* knowledge as ice floes on a dark sea; ignorance is the natural state. *P→I.* The "nuclear option" for a bored child. *Materials:* Cham & Whiteson *We Have No Idea*.

**Big History** — Use the whole arc — Big Bang → stars → life → all human cultures — as the backbone connecting every subject. *Hook:* "every culture has an origin story — except, perhaps, ours." *M→R→P.* *Materials:* Gonick *Cartoon History of the Universe*; Big History Project (David Christian).

**Unraveling Fears** — Each year the child names one fear; learn the psychology of fear, then run small personal experiments to shrink it and present the data. *Hook:* the hero must descend to the underworld; coming-of-age courage. *S+R(heroic)+P(experiment).* *Materials:* Jia Jiang *Rejection Proof*.

**Strange Sensations** — Isolate one sense (blindfold) and taste/feel/heft mystery objects, then name and describe each. *Hook:* the world is "prickly, silky, acrid" but kids grow up in sterile abstraction; art begins as conveying the ineffable. *S+M(games)+R(collections).* (Avoid the top-8 allergens.)

**Origin Stories (science & math)** — Introduce a big idea through the wild story of the person who found it. *Hook:* "K-12 content isn't information, it's history congealed" — to learn is to re-enact. *M→R(heroes)→P.* *Materials:* Joy Hakim *Story of Science*; Bryson *A Short History of Nearly Everything*; a "Wall of Saints" of portraits.

## Math

**Finger Math** — Teach the real finger-counting system that turns hands into an abacus (count to 99, then add/subtract/multiply). *Hook:* we make arithmetic too hard by forcing it through the prefrontal cortex; hands are cognitively privileged; the dream of "freaky mental math." *S→P.* *Materials:* Lieberthal *The Complete Book of Finger Math*.

**Bead Computers (soroban) / Basic Abacus** — Use a real soroban (or a 9-bead-per-rod Western abacus) in place of a calculator until the child flicks beads in their head. *Hook:* "no abacus, no civilization"; "'7' doesn't look like seven" — abstraction made physical. *S↔P.* *Materials:* a cheap soroban/abacus (store-bought 10-bead ones are "fake").

**Masters of Measurement** — Anchor every metric unit to a *felt* object (1 L ≈ a water bottle, 1 ML ≈ a pool), then do Fermi estimates ("how much does my school drink?"). *Hook:* our brains melt on unfamiliar scales — which blocks real environmental judgment. *S(felt)→P.* *Materials:* Orlin *Math for English Majors*; Munroe's radiation chart.

**The Magic of Numbers** (elementary, 3 threads) — **Lessons** (daily concept-ladder practice + Boss Problems + personifying numbers + symbol origin stories + a Deep Practice Book), **Puzzles** (ciphers, board games, jokes, narrativized classics like Tower of Hanoi), and a **Quixotic Goal** (freaky mental math). *Hook:* numbers are enchanted but taught cold; "what color does 7 want to be?" *M→P.* *Materials:* JUMP Math (Mighton); Prime Climb; Benjamin *Secrets of Mental Math*; 3Blue1Brown.

**Numbers Against Reality** (middle school) — Reframe around "the universe is written in mathematics": vivid images for the "-illions," a Deep Practice Book, cipher-cracking and proof "quests for glory." *Hook:* adolescent want → heroism; the Greek quest that birthed proof. *R(heroes/extremes)→P.* *Materials:* Netz *Why the Ancient Greeks Matter*; Hannah Fry.

**Concept Ladders (math)** — For finicky foundations, a tiny-step self-quizzing ladder (see TEACHING-PATTERNS). The "nuclear option" that ends school-as-failure. *S+M+P.* *Materials:* free downloadable ladders for algebra/exponents/roots.

**A Sneak Peek at Math** — The whole K-12 plan: elementary "magic of numbers" → middle "numbers against reality" (Fermi + Bayes) → high-school "pure math / philosophy of math" (infinity, Gödel). Spirals M→R→P→I three times. The master container for the math entries above.

## Language & Literacy

**Teach the IPA early** — Teach the relevant International Phonetic Alphabet as a "supertool" — to pronounce foreign names, do accents, and fix speech sounds. *Hook:* make kids language *freaks*; the cave-of-wonders of every sound a mouth can make. *S(sounds)+M(cipher)→P.* *Materials:* Wyner *Fluent Forever*; CrashCourse IPA video.

**Origins of the Alphabet** — Tell the alphabet's story as small grade-by-grade tales (why C *and* K; capital A = an upside-down ox head; the Greek vowel revolution). *Hook:* "English spelling was forged by Lucifer himself"; riddle-led. *M(stories/riddles)+R(humanizing).* *Materials:* *Ox, House, Stick*; Useful Charts poster.

**Other Alphabets / Mysterious Messages (ciphers)** — Post a weekly cryptic message — a non-Latin script, or a cipher that slowly hardens (Caesar → substitution → Vigenère) — and let the child decode it. Never grade, never explain, never reveal; keep it optional. *Hook:* "too much clarity is boring — kids seek secrets"; the heroic lineage Bacon→Babbage→Turing. *M(puzzle)+S(pattern)→P+R.* *Materials:* Gardner *Codes, Ciphers and Secret Writing*; cryptii.com.

**Strong Sentences** — Give clear sentence patterns to imitate (start "Cougars prowl"), discovering the grammar term *after* the pattern, using silly content. *Hook:* imitation of masters; whimsy spotlights structure. *S(imitation)→P.* *Materials:* *The Art of Styling Sentences*.

**Public Speaking** — Acclimate via very short speeches (30-60 sec at age 6, building to a few minutes); prompts drawn from the child's LiD topic; stand in a taped "magic box." *Hook:* cure the #1 phobia; the skills transfer everywhere. *M toolkit (jokes, metaphors, images).* *Materials:* Toastmasters' minute-timed format.

**Counting Rhymes** — Make little (deliberately bad) poems for skip-counting and times-tables, drafting them with ChatGPT. *Hook:* poems go back to the dawn of language; base-10 doesn't — play to our oldest strength. *M(rhythm)→P.* *Materials:* Schoolhouse Rock "Multiplication Rock."

**Learn a Second Language (family method)** — Five moves: taste a language by naming a pet in 9 tongues → *adopt* one as a family with a real trip booked → tune your tongues (IPA + minimal pairs) → "eat a thousand words" (image-based, English-free flashcards + spaced repetition) → a years-long family game of Taboo. *Hook:* a real trip as motivation; "how do my language and culture shape me?" *M(names/images)+S(sounds).* *Materials:* Wyner *Fluent Forever*; Anki; Zipf's-law word lists.

## History & Geography

**Know a Place** — Study one nearby feature (creek, hill) for *three years* — prehistory, then present ecology, then human history — aimed at one ambitious final presentation. *Hook:* "every spot is a door to Life, the Universe and Everything." *All stages.* *Materials:* Judson *A Walking Curriculum*; Google Earth.

**Geography by Heart** — Memorize the world from grade 1 (continents → countries → cities → coolest features → all UN nations), each place paired with a vivid image, fact, and story. *Hook:* "when we don't know where things are, we're stupid no matter how smart"; geography is the stage history happens on. *S→M→R/P.* *Materials:* Seterra.com; wall maps; Geeky Songs for mnemonics.

**Geography in Elementary** (3 threads) — **Maps** (bird's-eye role-play to teach what a map *is*), **Marvels** (one Atlas-Obscura wonder a day, made into memory cards), **Navigation** (cardinal-direction wall cards → pointing games → Sun/star navigation). *Hook:* marvels as the ancient gateway to the wider world. *M+S.* *Materials:* *Atlas Obscura Explorer's Guide for the Most Adventurous Kid*; Gooley *The Natural Navigator*.

**Spiral History** — Tell the whole world's story three times — elementary (simple stories), middle (complex stories), high school (meta-narratives) — each cycle split the same way. *Hook:* "the real voyage of discovery is having new eyes" (Proust). *M→R→P.* *Materials:* Bauer *Story of the World*; Gonick *Cartoon History*.

**Nested Timelines** — Four wall timelines at different scales (modern life → civilization → life on Earth → universe), shading where each sits on the next, plotting events from *every* subject as images. *Hook:* "cavemen didn't fight dinosaurs" — one timeline is never enough. *M(metaphor)+R(diagrams)→P.* *Materials:* ChronoZoom.com; DIY beats pre-made.

**Epic Stories** — Grades 1-8: the parent tells true, captivating history stories around a campfire setup (lights off, a YouTube fire), from every culture (Spartacus, Haitian Revolution, Ruby Bridges, Malala). *Hook:* "how did Lucas make $5 billion? an epic story"; belonging to all humanity. *M→R(heroic/ideals)→P→I.* (Avoid sugary sentimentality.) *Materials:* Bauer; Waldorf storytelling.

**Dueling Histories** — Tell major events from *opposing* perspectives (different characters → different sides → different ideologies). *Hook:* "every story tells a lie" — so surface bias, don't hide it; everyone loves a fight. *M(fight)+P→I.* *Materials:* Loewen *Lies My Teacher Told Me*; Zinn vs. Paul Johnson read against each other.

## Art & Music

**A Painting a Week** — Weekly, lead the child into one great artwork with a method — observe details, guess the story, mimic a figure's pose and imagine their senses, later tell the artist's story; keep each hung up "in play." *Hook:* an oil painting was our ancestors' "8K display"; a method beats "I'm not an art person." *S(mimicry)+M→R→P.* *Materials:* Visual Thinking Strategies; Travers *Touching the Art*.

**A Movie a Month** — Watch one *real* (never "educational") film as a group, chosen to be kid-enjoyable, meaningful to you, and tied to a topic; refer back for months. No quiz. *Hook:* movies yoke thinking to feeling — "what they give isn't rigor, it's richness." *S+M+R→P→I.* *Materials:* graded examples from *The Red Balloon* and *Totoro* to *The Truman Show* and *The Matrix*.

**A Song a Week** — Dig into one song across daily listens, then a "music station" menu of challenges — rhythm, mood-drawing, lyrics, the artist's story, critique, and creating a parody/mnemonic. *Hook:* "a good song is a qualia duplicator"; music crosses every divide. *S+M→R→P→I.*

**Geeky Songs** — Use songs that teach academic *details* (states, bones, π digits): read the lyrics, act them out, sing while marching. *Hook:* Aboriginal songlines prove songs encode hard facts; outdated science songs show how ideas evolve. *M→P.* *Materials:* They Might Be Giants; Schoolhouse Rock; AsapSCIENCE.

**Simple Doodles** — Get the child drawing *a lot* — cartoony doodles first (no rush to realism), diverse styles, "draw a fire hydrant a dozen ways." *Hook:* "if you're human, you can become good at drawing and love it"; to draw is to really see. *M→P→I.* *Materials:* Ed Emberley; Lee J. Ames *Draw 50…*.

**Making Noise / Mysterious Music** — Make noise with objects and the body and notice *why* materials sound as they do; expose the child to diverse instrumental music (gamelan, raga, whale song) and have them "draw sound" non-representationally, then compare drawings. *Hook:* "we're more like bats than we think"; the shock that others hear it differently. *S(musicality)→R→P.* *Materials:* Newman *MouthSounds*.

**Art of Memory** — Teach and routinely *use* the memory palace, link system, and major system to lock in useful sets. (See TEACHING-PATTERNS.) *Hook:* "weaponized foolishness." *S→.* *Materials:* Foer *Moonwalking with Einstein*.

## Philosophy, Virtue & Religion

**Philosophy Everywhere / with Preschoolers** — When real content prompts a big question, *honor it* — pause and discuss; don't run a separate program. For little ones, three games: Word Chain, interrogate One Object ("could a spoon be made of air?"), and Strange Situations ("a dog is found on Mars — how?"). *Hook:* every subject traces back to philosophy; kids already ask the Big Questions. *M(games)→P.* *Materials:* Hershovitz *Nasty, Brutish, and Short*; Philosophy for Children (Lipman).

**Named Virtues** — Choose a *handful* of defining values (not all 24); pursue each through stories and heroes, question them openly, and make visible sacrifices to uphold them — then let each "supercharge" every subject. *Hook:* "he who has a why can bear any how"; shared values make a group a superorganism. *M→R(heroes)→P→I.* *Materials:* Peterson & Seligman's 24 strengths; Haidt *The Righteous Mind*.

**World Proverbs** — A proverb a day (diverse origins): copy it (doubling as handwriting), restate it, debate it, memorize it, connect it. *Hook:* "the most experience in the fewest words"; *good to think with*. *M(microliterature)→P.* *Materials:* Stone *The Routledge Book of World Proverbs*.

**World Religions Everywhere** — Stop keeping religion out: include its stories, art, history, and questions across the curriculum (memorize doctrines young; visit houses of worship; debate big ideas later). *Hook:* "ignoring religion means ignoring how the world works." A speed-run of all five stages. *Materials:* Prothero *God Is Not One*; Huston Smith *The World's Religions*.

**Everyone Learns Law** — Monthly, drop a bizarre real case (the hot-coffee suit; the monkey selfie; "is a tomato a fruit?"; the cannibalism "Speluncean Explorers"): tell the human story, assign sides, argue, then reveal the real ruling. *Hook:* teens' naive passion for justice meets millennia of careful argument. *M/R→P→I+S(role-play).* *Materials:* "The Case of the Speluncean Explorers."

## Story & Play

**Playing inside Stories** (worked: an Anansi tale) — After telling a key story once, *return* to it repeatedly doing something new: re-enact with props; summarize in 3/2/1 sentences then 3 words; dissect the characters' wants; switch media (Lego, song, drawing); retell from another POV; recast with animals; change the setting, genre, ending, or binary. *Hook:* "treat every story as a jungle gym"; we forget what we skate over. *M(story)+S(play)→P(genre as model)→I.* *Materials:* Snyder *Save the Cat*; props (capes, foam swords, boxes).

**Telling Jokes** — Make jokes a real subject equal to spelling: read joke books, perform on stage, get feedback, argue about *why* a joke is funny, then write your own. *Hook:* "it takes brains to be funny" — untangling a joke *is* analytic thinking; school filters out the funny. *S(humor)→M→P(logic)→I.* *Materials:* comedy schools' methods; joke books "in the bathroom."

**Star Lore** — From kindergarten, teach the juicy traditional lore (easily-spotted constellations, the Zodiac, the Perseus/Andromeda myths); from upper elementary add modern lore (these are *suns*; see the sky in 3D; find the Andromeda galaxy). *Hook:* after 13 years most can only say "those are stars, very far away"; "story precedes science." *M(stories/images)+S→R(wonder)→P.* *Materials:* H.A. Rey *The Stars: A New Way to See Them* (the constellation lines actually resemble the figures); Sagan/Tyson *Cosmos*.

## Cross-cutting formats — drop *any* topic into these

These recur across every domain. They are the reusable templates the parent can apply to a brand-new subject.

1. **"A ___ a Week / Month / Day"** — a recurring, deepening encounter with a great work via a menu of challenges. *(A Song/Painting/Poem a Week, A Movie a Month, A Dinosaur a Day, a proverb a day.)* Apply to any rich canon: a Building a Week, a Recipe a Week, an Experiment a Week.

2. **"Know a ___"** — sustained, multi-sensory, long-duration attention to ONE local specimen until it becomes a beloved "person"/place. *(Know a Tree / a Place / Local Birds.)* This is Learning in Depth at home. Apply to any concrete observable thing nearby.

3. **"Origins of ___"** — teach a tool or convention via the quirky human story of who invented it and why it looks as it does. *(Origins of the Alphabet; math-symbol histories.)* Apply to the calendar, money, musical notation, anything.

4. **"___ Everywhere" / "Everyone Learns ___"** — refuse to silo a major human domain; weave it through every subject instead. *(Philosophy / World Religions Everywhere; Everyone Learns Law.)*

5. **"Your ___ is Wrong"** — open with a riddle exposing that the standard understanding is subtly false, then rebuild it with truer metaphors. *(Your Solar System is Wrong.)* Apply to any topic where folk-knowledge diverges from reality.

6. **Collections toward completion** — amass a memorable set (40 birds, 195 nations, the constellations, the spices), front-loading the coolest items, riding the collecting urge.

7. **Mysterious / withheld content** — present knowledge as a secret to be earned; never explain, never grade, let the mystery pull. *(Secrets & Revelations; ciphers.)*

8. **Dueling perspectives** — present one subject from two+ opposing viewpoints to surface (not hide) bias and provoke idea-fights. *(Dueling Histories; mock trials.)*

9. **Narrativize-in-4-phases** — wrap any practice in Orient → Complicate → Transform → Integrate (the learning arc in EGAN-TOOLKIT).

10. **Three-thread / spiral** — structure a whole subject as **Lessons** (rigorous core) + **Puzzles** (extracurricular delight) + a **Quixotic Goal** (one crazy aspirational feat), and re-run it every few years at a deeper stage.

11. **Eganize-an-LLM** — write a long prompt that forces ChatGPT to use the imaginative tools (personification, metaphor, story, emotional binary) rather than dry abstraction. *(Plant MatchMaker; IPA breakdowns; counting rhymes.)*

12. **Crowdsource-the-canon** — invite nominations (in a fixed format) for a ~100-item canon, judged against the multi-stage test. *(The World in 100 Stories; The Most Powerful Songs of All Time.)*
