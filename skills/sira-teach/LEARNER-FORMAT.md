# LEARNER.md Format

`LEARNER.md` lives at the workspace root when the family workspace serves one child. If the family uses Sira for more than one child, keep one profile per child in `learners/`.

Read the right learner profile before every lesson. It governs attention, reading level, tone, interests, sensitivities, and how the child currently shows understanding.

Do not ask the parent to classify the child with pedagogy labels. Ask plain questions, record plain evidence, and let Sira infer teaching tools silently.

## Template

```md
# Learner: {Child's name}

## Basics
- **Age**: {age}
- **Reads independently?**: {no / emerging / yes - with what kinds of text}
- **Writes or dictates comfortably?**: {what works now}
- **Focused activity window**: {e.g. 5 minutes, 10 minutes, 20 minutes}

## What lights them up
- {Current obsessions, characters, games, places, collections, hobbies, jokes, questions, songs, stories, animals, tools}

## What tends to work
- {Story, movement, drawing, maps, building, songs, jokes, acting, puzzles, quiet reading, conversation, experiments, outdoor noticing, etc.}

## What tends not to work
- {Formats, tones, topics, lengths, transitions, or pressures that usually fail}

## Subject notes
- **Geography**: {current level, spark, cautions}
- **History**: {current level, spark, cautions}
- **Mathematics**: {current level, spark, cautions}
- **Sciences**: {current level, spark, cautions}
- **Other subjects**: {only as needed}

## Sensitivities & cautions
- {Anything to avoid or handle gently - fears, frustrations, sensory issues, scary topics, perfectionism, shame triggers}

## How they show understanding
- {How this child reveals real understanding vs. parroting - retells in their own words, uses the idea in play, asks "but why", teaches someone else, draws it, builds it, notices it later}
```

## Rules

- **Ask in parent language.** "What lights them up?" beats "which tool of understanding is active?"
- **Infer internally.** Sira may reason about somatic, mythic, romantic, philosophic, ironic, or trivium-style patterns internally, but those labels do not belong in first-contact parent onboarding.
- **Sparks are gold.** Keep current fascinations visible and fresh.
- **Update from accrued signal.** New appetite for argument, extremes, making, reflection, or sustained fascination may signal a shift. Update here when the pattern is clear or the parent corrects you.
- **Keep it short and current.** This is a compass, not a case file. Prune stale obsessions.
- **Defer to the operator.** The parent knows the child better than Sira does.
