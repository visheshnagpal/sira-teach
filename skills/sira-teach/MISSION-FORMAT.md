# MISSION.md Format

`MISSION.md` usually lives inside a subject folder. It captures *why* this subject matters for this child or family right now. It has two halves: the **parent's hope** and the **child's doorway**. Every teaching decision in the subject should trace back here.

## Template

```md
# {Subject} Mission — for {child or family}

## The parent's hope
{1-3 sentences. The concrete real-world reason this matters. What changes for the child — what they'll be able to do, understand, or feel? Push past "to understand X" to the underlying outcome.}

## The child's doorway
{1-2 sentences. What already lights this child up that can open the subject? This may be a current fascination like Minecraft, maps, music, money, buildings, stories, games, family life, etc. If you don't know yet, say so.}

## Success looks like
- {A specific, observable thing the child will be able to do, tell, or make}
- {Another specific thing}

## Current arc
- **This year**: {what this year should cultivate}
- **This season or term**: {the current focus}
- **This week**: {the current rhythm or next move}

## Constraints
- {Attention span, time, budget, the child's stage, format preferences — anything that bounds the approach}

## Out of scope
- {Adjacent things to not chase right now — protects the zone of proximal development}
```

## Rules

- **One mission per subject.** A family workspace can contain many subject missions.
- **Concrete over abstract.** "Read a chapter book aloud by spring" beats "get better at reading." "Plant and harvest one vegetable" beats "learn about plants."
- **The child's doorway often beats the parent's hope as a hook.** Lead lessons through the doorway when it is real, while preserving the subject's deeper mission.
- **Interview the operator if it's vague.** A child's mission is often the parent's hope plus a spark the parent can name better than you. Ask. A bad mission is worse than none.
- **Revise from accrued signal.** Missions move as children grow. Update this file when repeated evidence or an explicit parent correction warrants it.
- **Keep it short.** Past one screen, it has stopped being a compass and become a curriculum.
