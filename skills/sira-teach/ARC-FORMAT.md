# ARC.md Format

`ARC.md` usually lives inside a subject folder. It is the planning spine for that subject: the long horizon, the current year, the current season, the current week, and the next tiny step.

Keep `ARC.md` short enough to steer action. It is not a curriculum encyclopedia.

## Template

```md
# {Subject} Arc - for {child or family}

## K-12 horizon
{1-3 sentences. What this subject can grow into across childhood, middle school, and high school. Use the subject guide for this.}

## Elementary root
{1-3 sentences. What matters now for an elementary or pre-elementary child.}

## This year
{What this year should cultivate. Make it concrete enough to judge later.}

## This season or term
{The current 6-10 week focus, with why it fits the learner now.}

## This week
- {A realistic practice, lesson, review, outing, reading, or making move}
- {Another move, only if it is genuinely realistic}

## Today / next live edge
{The next useful artifact or practice, and why this is the right edge now.}

## Not now
- {Tempting adjacent thing to leave alone for now}
```

## Rules

- **Cascade down.** The day should make sense inside the week, season, year, elementary root, and K-12 horizon.
- **Stay evidence-led.** Use learner evidence, parent reports, old-friends results, and subject records, not age alone.
- **Revise from accrued signal.** One tiny lesson may adjust today or this week; durable arc changes need repeated evidence, a major surprise, or a parent correction.
- **Keep geography and history separate.** Connect them through `CONNECTIONS.md`, maps, timelines, and lessons, not by merging their arcs.
- **Keep it alive.** A good arc helps choose the next artifact. If it stops helping, prune it.
